## Core Understanding
Mira is the story’s central protagonist, a young woman burdened by a midnight letter from her deceased mother and a mysterious silver key she recovered from Saint Alder Chapel years ago. In late June 1998 she and Arun investigate the chapel, confronting hidden family ties to Elias Vale, unsettling revelations from Sister Celine, and the weight of secrets that drive her toward the river path.

## Stable Profile
- - Role: Protagonist; primary driver of the investigation into the chapel’s mysteries.
- - Relationships: Arun (close confidant and fellow greenhouse worker), Nani (grandmother who painted the blue pot, now deceased), Mother (deceased, left the letter), Elias Vale (mother’s brother, missing by the river, linked to Mira’s past), Sister Celine (enigmatic nun who knows about the key).
- - Possessions: Silver key (found under chapel floorboards at age 13, now carried in apron pocket), Mother’s letter (arrived 11:40 p.m. on June 26, 1998), three river stones, cracked watch (stopped at 2:12 a.m.), torn toll‑house ledger page naming Elias as her mother’s brother, packet of letters bound in black thread.
- - Recurring associations: Saint Alder Chapel, the greenhouse (blue pot with white cranes), the river path, the toll‑house ledger.
- - Traits: Haunted by family secrets, meticulous about keeping the key, emotionally volatile (hands shake when holding tea), driven by a need to uncover truth, cautious yet willing to act.

## Interpretation
- - The precise timing (5:40 a.m., 6:05 a.m., 7:15 a.m.) underscores Mira’s urgency and the narrowing window before daylight fully reveals the chapel’s secrets.
- - Her lie about the letter’s content (“No”) followed by an immediate confession reveals a growing internal conflict between protecting Arun and confronting her own past.
- - The silver key’s re‑emergence signals Mira’s acceptance that the past cannot stay buried; it becomes both a literal and symbolic lock she feels compelled to open.
- - Sister Celine’s accusation that Mira “should not have brought the key here” hints that the key is a trigger for something dangerous, raising Mira’s stakes.
- - The discovery that Elias is her mother’s brother, not a cousin, reshapes Mira’s family narrative and intensifies her personal stake in the mystery.

## Element-Centered Chronology
### Before current narrative
- At age 13, Mira discovered a silver key hidden beneath the floorboards of Saint Alder Chapel and kept it secret.
- She remembers Elias Vale, her mother’s brother, warning her never to speak of the toll‑house ledger.

### Chapter 7 — Late June 1998
- 5:40 a.m.: Mira arrives at the greenhouse, receives her mother’s midnight letter (11:40 p.m.) and feels old dread resurfacing.
- She lies to Arun about the letter’s content, then admits it mentions “the one who went missing by the river.”
- She shows Arun the silver key she has been carrying again for three nights; Arun remarks on the key’s significance.
- Mira recalls a June morning ten years earlier when Elias stood on the chapel path, urging her silence about the ledger.
- At 6:05 a.m. they leave the greenhouse, heading toward Saint Alder Chapel with the letter, key, and unspoken names.

### Chapter 8 — June 28, 1998
- 7:15 a.m.: Mira and Arun reach Saint Alder Chapel; the bells are silent but the tower rope still sways.
- Mira discovers fresh muddy footprints leading to the altar and a cloth bundle containing three river stones, a cracked watch (stopped at 2:12 a.m.), and a torn ledger page naming Elias as her mother’s brother.
- Sister Celine appears, accusing Mira of bringing the key; Mira feels the key in her pocket and realizes its dangerous relevance.
- After a brief search, the bundle and Sister Celine vanish; Mira takes the watch, letters, and ledger page, sensing another presence inside the chapel.
- 7:32 a.m.: They exit via the side door, avoiding the toll house, and head toward the river path, the morning air feeling darker than before.
